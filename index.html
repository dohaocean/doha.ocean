Kelvin:

Kelvin grew up in Guangzhou, China.

He is becoming the best of himself so as to contribute to a happier world.

Kelvin is now a PhD candidate at Columbia University studying Geophysics.

He is excited in building physical models to study the dynamics of the Earth, by applying knowledge and tools from Applied Mathematics, Mechanical Engineering, Earth Sciences and Computer Sciences. For example, the models were built with knowledges from all above-mentioned disciplines and were simulated by a parallelized code (SNAC) with the world-fastest supercomputers (Tianhe-2 and Stampede) in order to better understand the fascinating Mid-Ocean Ridges systems, the longest mountain ranges that are buried under ~3 km of ocean where volcanic eruptions and earthquakes are happening right at this moment. He dreams about predicting earthquakes one day.
